# Marketing Campaign Optimization: A/B Testing & ROI Analysis 🛒📈

This project analyzes the effectiveness of two email marketing campaigns (A and B) for **FreshMart**, an online grocery store. Using statistical inference and business metrics, I determined which strategy maximizes conversion and financial return.

## 🎯 Business Challenge
The goal was to move beyond "vanity metrics" (like open rates) and identify which campaign truly drives revenue. I applied A/B testing methodology to ensure that performance differences were statistically significant and not just random fluctuations.

## 🧪 Statistical Methods Applied
- **Chi-Square Test of Independence:** To determine if the campaign type significantly influences purchase behavior.
- **Independent T-Test:** To compare the average revenue generated by each campaign.
- **Z-Test for Proportions:** To validate the difference in conversion rates between groups.
- **Business KPIs:** Calculation of **ROI (Return on Investment)** and **CPA (Cost per Acquisition)**.

## 📈 Key Insights from Data
* **Conversion Champion:** Campaign B achieved a statistically significant higher conversion rate ($p < 0.05$).
* **Revenue Performance:** The T-test confirmed a measurable difference in the financial value generated per customer interaction.
* **Financial Efficiency:** Beyond statistical significance, I calculated the **ROI** to determine the actual profitability of the marketing spend.

## 🚀 Strategic Recommendations
1. **Scale Campaign B:** Data shows it is the superior strategy for driving conversions and should be prioritized in future budgets.
2. **Optimize Design:** Analyze subject lines and promotional messaging from Campaign B to replicate its success.
3. **Data-Driven Roadmap:** Continue using A/B testing for all major marketing changes to ensure evidence-based decision-making.
